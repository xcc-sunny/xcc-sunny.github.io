ReCF: Exploiting Response Reasoning for Correlation Filters in Real-Time UAV Tracking

Fuling Lin, Changhong Fu*, Yujie He, Weijiang Xiong
2020-03-06

Abstract

The high eﬃciency of correlation ﬁlter (CF) based trackers has led to the widespread development of online unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) object tracking. This kind of method can eﬀectively train a ﬁlter to discriminate the target from the background using limited samples. When utilizing historical information, most methods assume a ﬁxed label function over all the previous samples. The assumption can easily lead to over-ﬁtting and ﬁlter degradation, especially in complex drone scenarios. To address this problem, we propose a novel response reasoning for correlation ﬁler learning (ReCF), which can leverage temporal information in ﬁlter training and signiﬁcantly promote the robustness of the tracker. The proposed response reasoning approach goes beyond the conventional response consistency assumption and constructs an auxiliary label of the current sample. The proposed method helps learn a generic relationship between the previous and current ﬁlters, which can realize self-regulated ﬁlter updating and enhance the discriminability of the ﬁlter. Extensive experiments on three well-known UAV tracking benchmarks, i.e., UAV123@10fps, DTB70, and UAVDT, show that our method yields superior results with real-time performance on a single CPU, which can be eﬀectively applied to UAV online tracking tasks.
